Here's the breakdown:

  • 1 Assigns the div to the variable d
  • 2 Counts the number of paragraphs
  • 3 Goes through the children of the div looking for the next element node,i.e. Type 1, which we assume is a paragraph
  • 4-6 Adds 1 to the counter. We're looking for the second paragraph
  • 7-9 When the counter hits 2, we've reached the target paragraph.Change that second paragraph, it would be a lot easier to assign it an id, and go straight to it,like this.
    document.getElementById("p2").innerHTML = "All his men.";

You can see why the getElementId method is more popular with coders than tracing the DOM hierarchy. But there are some things you can't do without working your way through the parent-child relationships of the DOM
